Climate Action, In Action

Mapping the Present to Protect the Future

Conservation efforts and AI capibilities have converged to create GaiaEye, a mapping software that provides the boots on the ground with information they need to effectively make sustainable and effecient decisions. With image recognition and geolocational plotting, conservationists can use GaiaEye's map to better understand their sites and monitor progress.

How does it work, exactly?

Gaia Eye combines the use of UAV (drone) photography, image recognition, and geolocation data to provide the user with a regularly updated, dynamic map of European Buckthorn in an area. With a simple setup using a DJI drone, GaiaEye takes multiple photos of a given area and analyzes them with our algorithm. When identified, our software plots each instance on a map that is available to the user.

Our Vision, from acorn to oak

A new initiative of All-American Gardens, Gaia Eye will incorporate emerging technologies to assist in the restoration of our green spaces. Our software can monitor, identify, and inform for more effective stewardship. Founded in 2016, All-American Gardens reintroduced native species and help strengthen urban ecosystems in Indianapolis' East Side. Through intentional orchard management, targeted planting, and partnerships with city-wide sustainability experts, our organization has established two urban prairies, two fruit orchards, and a hazelnut patch. With the development and deployment of Gaia Eye, All-American Gardens can further its impact to branch out of the urban niche and move into the broader world of habitat restoration. Gaia Eye's realization is thanks in large part to another public partnership with the Forest Preserves of Cook County. Developed in the field and with the sole permission of the FPCC, our software is field-tested and Mother Nature approved. Special thanks go out to John Balaban, Michael Andrews, and Doctor Sayani Sarkar, without whose guidance Gaia Eye would have never come to fruition.

Leaving no Leaf Unturned

For years, volunteer efforts have made slow, incredmental progress towards eradicating invasives and bolstering native environments. With GaiaEye generated maps, these volunteers can target their attention to invasive flare-ups and monitor previous areas to measure intentional seeding efforts.
