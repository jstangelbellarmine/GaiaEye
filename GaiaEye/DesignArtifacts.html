Personas

Juan Vallavin Balaban, Site Steward, Retired Teacher

Juan is a 67-year-old site steward who has been managing ecological restoration projects for over 3 decades. Every workday morning, he starts his day at 7 AM by contacting fire monitoring services to confirm site conditions. With over 100 acres to manage, he carefully selects controlled burn sites and plants for restoration. Using Gaia Eye, he maps out restoration efforts, prioritizes high-risk areas, and coordinates volunteer workdays.

Miguel Andres, Site Steward

Miguel is a conservationist in his late 60s who supervises restoration sites for a the North Branch of the Cook County Forest Preserve. He relies on Gaia Eye to monitor invasive species regrowth and ensure previous restoration efforts are effective. By using drone-based imagery and AI-driven classification, he can assess large areas quickly and plan targeted removal operations.

Laura Larkeur, Researcher

Dr. Laura Larkeur is a professor specializing in machine learning applications in remote sensing. She has researched the effectiveness of CNNs for ecological monitoring and recommended the use of YOLOv11 for Gaia Eye. Her expertise helps refine the AI model, improving detection accuracy for invasive species.

Jose San Angel, volunteer

Jose is a passionate computer scientist in his mid-30s, leading the development of Gaia Eye. He has taken a special interst in AI and applied machine learning, ensuring that the software remains efficient and scalable. With a love for nature and technology, he is dedicated to creating a tool that empowers conservationists.

Tomaso Wells, esteemed professor

Tomaso is a university professor in his 30s who specializes in ecology and environmental science. He prefers lab work over fieldwork, but recognizes Gaia Eye’s potential in conservation research. He encourages his students to use the tool for real-world ecological studies.

User Stories

As a site steward, I want to map invasive species locations so I can better plan restoration efforts.


As a conservationist, I need real-time updates on restoration progress so I can see what works.


As a Work Day Leader, I want to use Gaia Eye’s data to schedule workdays where help is most needed.


As a researcher, I need AI-classified plant data to study the success of ecological interventions.


As a homeowner, I want to monitor my property remotely to track the spread of invasive species.


As a drone operator, I need an easy way to capture and upload images for analysis.


As an environmental professor, I want to integrate Gaia Eye into coursework for hands-on conservation studies.


As a student, I want to use Gaia Eye to analyze real-world ecological data for research projects.


As a policy maker, I want to use Gaia Eye’s reports to support funding for habitat restoration initiatives.


As a developer, I need an intuitive interface that allows conservationists with limited experience to use Gaia Eye.


Feature List

As a site manager, I want Gaia Eye to generate an up-to-date map of invasive species locations.


As a researcher, I want high-resolution drone imagery analyzed with AI to classify species.


As a conservationist, I need real-time monitoring of restoration efforts.


As a developer, I want seamless integration between Gaia Eye’s database, AI model, and mapping tools.


Constraints

We are limited by available drone hardware and the accuracy of AI classification models.


We must consider accessibility for non-technical conservationists using the platform.
